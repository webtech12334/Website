Earth

About Earth:

The third Planet from the Sun, Earth is the only known object in our Universe which harbours life. According to most scientific research, Earth formed around 4.5 billion years ago, and has only one natural satellite known simply as the moon. The gravitational and magnectic fields of Earth interact directly with other celestrial bodies like the moon and Sun.

General Information

  • Number of moons: 1
  • Date Of Discovery: Unknown
  • Rotation Period: 24hrs
  • Planetary Type: Terestrial
  • Distance From Sun: 149.6 million km
  • Atmosphere: Primarily Carbon Dioxide and Oxygen
  • Name: Derived from a personified goddess in Germanic paganism

Enviromental/Climactic Features:

The ocean tides on Earth are largely controlled by the gravitational Interaction between the Earth and moon. This also allows the Earth to stabalise its orientation on its axis, and gradurally slow in rotation. Although Earth is not the largest planet in our Solar System, it is the densest planet, and is the largest of all the Terestrial planets (Mars, Venus, Mercury and Earth).
