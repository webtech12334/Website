About Planets

A planet is a celestrial body which moves in an eliptical orbit around a Star. This object is large enough to become rounded by its own gravity, but is not large enough to cause thermoneuclear fusion, and subsequently ignite into a Star. In fact, there are more planets than Stars in our galaxy, with a current count of eight planets in our own Solar System. While the environments of these plants range from being made up of gas, to having temperatures well below freezing, or well above the melting point of most of our current metals, it remains debatable about how large a celestrial body has to be, in order to be classified as a planet.

A variety of space agencies, most notably NASA and Spacex, have set their sights on the planet Mars, as the first destination for a manned mission to another planet. This is because among all the plants in our solar System, Mars is the closest to Earth in both distance (with the exception of venus), and environmental/climactic features. It is therefore believed to be our best opportunity for colonisation.

EXOPLANETS: EXTRASOLAR PLANETS

What are Exoplanets:

These are planets outside of our solar system which orbit Stars other than our own. Some of these planets contain atmospheres that are thought to support life. This is usually predicted when the planet orbits at a distance from its Star that allows liquid water to exist in a similar condition to our own.

How are Exoplanets found:

The most common method of searching for Extrasolar planet around a Star is to identify whether the Star has a 'Wobbly' orbit. This phenomenon occurs when a planet orbits a star because its gravitational influence prevents the star from orbiting perfectly along its centre. However, this method is primarily used to detect planets the size of Jupiter. Those the size of Earth have little influence on the Stars orbit, so produce only a minor 'wobble'.

How many Exoplanets:

As of March 2019, a total of 4016 exoplanets have been successfully identified, most of which were discovered by the Kepler Telescope. However, not all of these planets are thought to be Earth-like. Many of them likely have conditions that are too extreme to support life, especially that of Human colonisation.

Notable Examples:

Name: Kepler-16b

Year Of Discovery: 15th September 2011

Information: Kepler-16b is a planet with a similar mass to Saturn. The planet consists of half gas and half rock and ice. Kepler-16b was the first planet to be identified as 'circumbinary', in that it orbits not one, but two Stars. This Binary Star system was first discovered by NASAs Kepler spacecraft.

Name: Proxima-centuri-b

Date Of Discovery: 26th August 2016

Information: Proxima-b is an exoplanet that orbits the Star proxmia Centuri. Due to the Stars small size relative to others, it is termed a 'red dwarf' Star. Proxmia-b is thought to orbit in the 'habital zone' of its Star, meaning it is likely at a distance that could support life. Upon discovery, it was identified as the closet exoplanet to our solar system, with a distance of 4.24 light years from Earth.

Name: Gliese-581c

Date Of Discovery: 4th April 2007

Information: Gliese-581c is an exoplanet with a mass 5.5 times of Earth, and is therefore classified as a super Earth. The notability of this planet is that its the only Earth-like planet in its Star system. Its temperature is within ranges thought to allow liquid water. However, further research has suggested the existence of life or the possiblity for human colonisation is unlikely. This is because the planet is tidally locked (also faces the same direction to the star), and so the best chance of life surviving would be in the 'Terminator zone'; an area on the planet that presents the division between light and dark.

Name: Kepler-22b

Date Of Discovery: 5th December 2011

Information: Roughly twice the size of Earth, Kepler-22b is thought to be an oceanic planet, existing within the habital zone of its host Star Kepler-22, which is slightly smaller and cooler than our own sun. However, given that Kepler orbits slightly closer to its Star than the Earth does the sun, it has been estaimted as hight as 95% chance that the planet is within the empirical habital zone.
