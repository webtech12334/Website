Jupiter

About Jupiter:

The fifth Planet from the Sun, Jupiter is the largest Planet in our Solar System. In fact, Jupiter is so large that its mass is two-and-half times greater than all the other planets in the Solar System combined. Jupiter is the third brightest object in the sky, after Venus and our Moon. The planet is primarily made of hydrogen and helium but lacks a solid a surface like the other gas planets. The most distinctive feature of Jupiter is the 'great red spot', which is an violent storm.

Mass, Size and Density:

The extreme size of Jupiter is seen in that its mass is 2.5 times greater than all the other Planets in the Solar System combined. However, although it is so much larger than Earth, due to its gaseos properties, it is significantly less dense than Earth, yet has a volume of approx 1,321 Earths and a radius one 10th of the Suns. A 'Jupiter Mass' is now a unit used to describe the size of other celestrial bodies like exoplanets (planets outside our Solar System). Researchers suggest that if Jupiter had any more mass it would srink as its interior compresses under the intense pressure. If this process was to continue, Jupiter would undergo 'stellar ignition', where a celestrial body ignites into a Star due to its intense pressure and compressed interior.

Interaction with the Solar System:

The extreme size of Jupiter means that its gravitational influence has helped form some of the features of the Solar System. In fact, all other planets in our Solar System, excluding Mercury, are closer to Jupiters orbital plane than they are the Suns equitorial plane. The extreme size also means that the center of gravity by which the Planet orbits the Sun lies just above the Suns surface. Jupiter is the only Planet in the Solar System where this is true.

The Great Red Spot

The most well known feature of Jupiter is the Great Red Spot. This is in fact a rapid storm that is larger than Earth itself. Researchers know that this storm has existed from atleast 1831, and possibly as early as 1665. This storms abnormal size means it has a complete rotation every 6 days. Although research has suggested that the storm is a stable permanent feature of Jupiter, the size has reduced significantly since it was discovered; leading some researchers to believe it will eventually cease to exist. The storm was first identified to be approx 25,500 miles across and last observations have measured the storm at approx 10,160 miles across. Showing more than a 50% reduction in size. However, this is not the only storm present on Jupiter. The white and brown overs are also lesser storms that have not be named.

Facts about Jupiter:

  • The lesser, white and brown, storms on Jupiter can last a few hours or even centuries
  • The ancient Babylonians (approx 7th-8th century) were the first to record observation of Jupiter
  • Jupiter also has a 'Great White Spot' that is larger than its Red counterpart but is less distinctive

Exploration:

Since 1973 there have been a number of SpaceCraft sent to study Jupiter and its moons. The first to be successful was the 'Pioneer 10' which sent back the first clear images of the Planet. In order to reduce the amount of energy, and therefore cost, to reach Jupiter, most probes use 'Gravity assist' to increase the speed and 'slingshot' the spacecraft with the Planets gravity. However, this increase the duration of the mission as the SpaceCraft takes a longer route to intercept and slingshot with other planets.

Past, Present and Future missions to Jupiter:

  • The Galileo spacecraft was the first to orbit Jupiter when it arrived in December 7, 1995. It orbited the Planet for seven years collecting data on its properties and its moons before parachuting into the planets atmosphere to collect and send back as much data as possible before the signal was lost and the probe would be vaporised.
  • Juno is an ongoing mission by NASA which arrived at Jupiter on July 4, 2016 and is expected to orbit the Planet 37 times over the next few months, collecting data on the Planets environment and moons.
  • The Jupiter Icy Moon Exploorer (JUICE) is the next planned mission in 2022, and will be followed the the 'Europa Clippper' in 2025
